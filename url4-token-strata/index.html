What the stratification shows. Tokenization is a genuine cross-model confound, but token count alone does not determine reachability. The direction and size of within-model differences vary by tokenizer and sampler.
What not to infer. A two-token word under Mistral is not the same statistical population as a two-token word under Gemma. These panels diagnose segmentation sensitivity; they do not create tokenizer-independent model rankings.
Confidence intervals use target-word cluster bootstrap within each available token-count stratum.
